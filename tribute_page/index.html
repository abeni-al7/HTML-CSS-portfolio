A tribute for Albert Einstein

A world renowned physicist

Here is a timeline of Albert Einstein's life:

  • March 14, 1879: Albert Einstein is born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire.
  • 1880: The Einstein family moves to Munich.
  • 1884: Albert starts his formal education at the Luitpold Gymnasium in Munich.
  • 1889: Begins receiving private lessons from Max Talmud, who introduces him to key scientific and philosophical texts.
  • 1894: The Einstein family moves to Italy. Albert stays briefly in Munich to finish his schooling but leaves to join his family.
  • 1895: Fails the entrance exam to the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich but excels in the math and physics sections.
  • 1896: Completes his secondary education at the Cantonal School in Aarau, Switzerland.
  • 1896: Enrolls at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich.
  • 1900: Graduates with a teaching diploma in physics and mathematics from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic.
  • 1902: Takes a position at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern as a technical expert.
  • 1903: Marries Mileva Marić, a fellow physics student from the Polytechnic.
  • 1905: Publishes four groundbreaking papers in the Annalen der Physik (Annus Mirabilis Papers), introducing the theory of special relativity and the famous equation E=mc².
  • 1908: Becomes a lecturer at the University of Bern.
  • 1909: Appointed associate professor at the University of Zurich.
  • 1911: Becomes a full professor at the German University in Prague.
  • 1912: Returns to the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich as a professor.
  • 1914: Moves to Berlin and becomes a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences.
  • 1915: Completes the general theory of relativity.
  • 1919: General relativity is confirmed during a solar eclipse, making Einstein an international celebrity.
  • 1921: Receives the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, not for relativity.
  • 1933: Leaves Germany due to the rise of the Nazi regime and moves to the United States. Takes a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1936: His first wife, Mileva Marić, dies.
  • 1936: Marries his cousin Elsa Löwenthal.
  • 1940: Becomes a U.S. citizen.
  • 1952: Is offered the presidency of Israel, which he declines.
  • April 18, 1955: Dies in Princeton, New Jersey, from an abdominal aortic aneurysm.

"He was almost wholly without sophistication and wholly without worldliness... There was always with him a wonderful purity at once childlike and profoundly stubborn."

--J. Robert Oppenheimer, a renowned physicist and the scientific director of the Manhattan Project
